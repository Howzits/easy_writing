# Article Quality Rubric

Score only after diagnosis. Cite a passage or clearly identified omission for every deduction. Treat the score as a decision aid, not an objective measurement. If the complete source is unavailable, label all scores provisional and state the limitation.

## Dimensions and Weights

### Thinking quality — 40 points

#### 1. Core proposition — 10

- **Diagnose:** Can the question, conclusion, and evidence route be stated compactly (about 100 Chinese characters or equivalent)? Do all sections serve that proposition?
- **Warnings:** No determinate question; competing conclusions; title, argument, and ending promise different things; evidence route cannot be reconstructed.
- **Revise:** State one question and one bounded conclusion, map the minimum evidence route, then remove or relocate material that does not serve it.

#### 2. Common-sense validity — 10

- **Diagnose:** Do claims survive basic tests of value, cost, incentives, causality, alternatives, and who pays? Are correlation and cause distinguished?
- **Warnings:** Causal reversal; universal language from narrow examples; ignored counterexamples, trade-offs, or stakeholders; factual premise presented without support.
- **Revise:** Qualify the claim, test alternative explanations and boundary cases, correct or verify factual premises, and expose the causal steps.

#### 3. Knowledge value — 10

- **Diagnose:** Does the article deliver **What** (a precise claim or distinction), **Why** (an explanatory cause or mechanism), or **How** (an executable method)?
- **Warnings:** Mostly **Wow**—importance, emotion, slogans, novelty claims, or grand conclusions without transferable knowledge; familiar ideas merely renamed.
- **Revise:** Replace Wow language with a precise distinction, causal explanation, decision rule, or usable procedure; add conditions and limits.

#### 4. Essence over phenomena — 10

- **Diagnose:** Does one mechanism explain multiple observations? Does the article show why the listed phenomena belong together?
- **Warnings:** A catalogue of examples treated as explanation; surface resemblance mistaken for shared cause; labels substituted for mechanisms.
- **Revise:** Name and test the mechanism, connect each phenomenon through explicit causal steps, and remove examples the mechanism cannot explain.

### Reader bridge — 35 points

#### 5. Reader empathy — 10

- **Diagnose:** Does the article anticipate missing context, unfamiliar terms, surprising claims, objections, and the reader's likely next question?
- **Warnings:** Undefined concepts; assumed insider knowledge; abrupt claims; examples meaningful only to the author; no orientation for stakes or audience.
- **Revise:** Add the minimum context, define terms at first use, answer likely objections, and sequence information from known to new.

#### 6. Logical integrity — 15

- **Diagnose:** Do the article goal, section functions, paragraph order, and sentence transitions form a coherent path? Are reasoning steps complete?
- **Warnings:** Sections drift from the goal; conclusions arrive before premises; missing warrants; circular reasoning; abrupt transitions or contradictory claims.
- **Revise:** Assign each section one function, restore missing premises and warrants, reorder by dependency, and add transitions that state the relationship.

#### 7. Knowledge packaging — 10

- **Diagnose:** Does the reader leave with a memorable and reusable concept, model, test, or method? Is it accurately named and bounded?
- **Warnings:** Insight remains diffuse; takeaway is only a slogan; framework categories overlap; advice cannot guide a future decision.
- **Revise:** Package the insight as a compact distinction, model, checklist, or decision rule; define when it applies and when it does not.

### Written expression — 25 points

#### 8. Showing through evidence — 10

- **Diagnose:** Are abstract judgments grounded in data, observable actions, dialogue, concrete examples, or scenes? Does the evidence support the exact claim?
- **Warnings:** Adjectives and conclusions stand in for proof; generic description without observable detail; hypothetical illustration presented as fact; anecdote overgeneralized.
- **Revise:** Replace description with evidence-based depiction: show the relevant action, number, quote, comparison, or scene; label hypotheticals and claims requiring verification.

#### 9. Concision — 10

- **Diagnose:** Does every section perform a necessary function? Can repetition, decorative filler, or redundant examples be removed without losing meaning or rhythm?
- **Warnings:** Repeated conclusion; multiple examples proving the same point; throat-clearing; abstract filler; avoidable detours.
- **Revise:** Keep the strongest formulation and evidence, combine repeated functions, cut decorative abstraction, and preserve deliberate rhetorical repetition only when its benefit is clear.

#### 10. Natural humor — 5

- **Diagnose:** When the purpose and tone call for humor, does it emerge from insight, accurate contrast, timing, or voice while preserving clarity and respect?
- **Warnings:** Inserted memes or jokes; humor obscures the claim, dates the piece, targets vulnerable people, or clashes with genre and audience.
- **Revise:** Derive humor from a truthful contrast or specific observation, reduce explanation after the punch, or remove it when it competes with trust.
- **N/A:** If humor is not called for, mark this dimension `N/A`; do not deduct points. Redistribute its 5 points proportionally within written expression: showing through evidence becomes 12.5 and concision 12.5, keeping the layer at 25 and the total at 100.

## Score Bands

- **90–100:** Publication-ready; only targeted polishing remains.
- **75–89:** Strong foundation; several important revisions remain.
- **60–74:** Readable, but structural or reasoning weaknesses limit impact.
- **Below 60:** Rebuild the proposition or structure before line editing.

## Severity

- **Critical:** Invalid core claim, unsupported central conclusion, severe factual or causal error, or an indeterminate article goal.
- **Major:** Broken structure, missing reasoning steps, extensive Wow content, absent reader context, or evidence too weak for an important claim.
- **Minor:** Wordiness, local ambiguity, weak transition, repetitive example, or style inconsistency.
- **Optional:** Taste-based polish that does not affect correctness or comprehension.

Order findings by dependency: proposition and facts, reasoning, structure, reader comprehension, evidence, concision, then style.

## Worked Finding

- **Evidence:** “三个客户抱怨后，转化率一定会提升。”
- **Diagnosis (Major):** The sentence turns a hypothetical sequence into a guaranteed causal result without evidence or intermediate reasoning.
- **Impact:** Readers cannot distinguish illustration from verified proof and may reject the central claim.
- **Action:** Label the scenario hypothetical, remove certainty, and state what measurement would test the claim.
- **Replacement:** “例如（假设场景），团队可在收到同类反馈后修改注册页，并比较改版前后的完成率，以检验这一问题是否影响转化。”
